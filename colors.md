Looking at this dashboard design, here's a detailed color analysis for your frontend implementation:
Primary Brand Colors
Blue

Main brand identity color used for interactive elements
Applied to circular icon backgrounds throughout the interface
Used in avatar circles with user initials
Creates visual hierarchy and draws attention to key actions

Coral/Pink (#FF6B7A or similar)

Secondary accent color for emphasis
Used for primary headings and section titles
Applied to numerical highlights and important metrics
Creates warmth and energy in the interface

Blue (#3B82F6)

Primary CTA button color
Provides strong contrast against the purple elements
Used for high-priority actionable items
Signals clickable, interactive elements

Functional Colors
Light Blue (#E0F2FE or similar)

Background for selected/active navigation states
Creates subtle highlight without overwhelming the interface
Indicates current page or section

Success Green (appears in positive metric indicators)

Used for upward trends and positive changes
Applied sparingly to maintain impact
Reinforces positive outcomes

Error/Negative Red (#EF4444 range)

Indicates cost reduction or negative deltas
Used with strikethrough effects
Signals areas of improvement or savings

Neutral Colors
White (#FFFFFF)

Card backgrounds for all metric containers
Main content area background
Creates clean separation and breathing room

Light Gray (#F3F4F6 to #E5E7EB)

Sidebar background color
Subtle borders on cards and containers
Dividers between sections

Dark Gray (#1F2937 to #374151)

Primary text color for headings and labels
Navigation menu items
Ensures strong readability and hierarchy

Medium Gray (#6B7280)

Secondary descriptive text
Timestamps and metadata
Supporting information that shouldn't compete with primary content

Usage Strategy
Cards: White backgrounds with thin light gray borders (#E5E7EB), purple gradient circular icons, and coral accent numbers create a clean, modern aesthetic
Buttons:

Primary actions use solid blue (#3B82F6) fills
Secondary actions use outlined style with blue borders
Tertiary actions may use text-only with hover states

Status badges:

Active/positive states use light green backgrounds (#D1FAE5) with dark green text (#065F46)
Create clear visual distinction for status information

Data visualization: Coral/pink accent highlights top-tier metrics and important scores, drawing the eye to key performance indicators
This color scheme balances professionalism (blues, grays) with energy and approachability (purples, corals), creating a modern SaaS dashboard aesthetic that's both functional and visually engaging.
